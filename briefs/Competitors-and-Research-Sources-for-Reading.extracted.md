# Competitors and Research Sources for Reading-to-Audio ADHD Project
## Direct Competitors (PDF → Audio / Reading → Audio)
These are products that already offer similar "turn documents into audio" functionality.
- **Transmonkey PDF to Audio** – Chrome extension that converts PDFs into natural-sounding MP3 for accessibility, learning, and on-the-go listening. [PDF to Audio - Chrome Web Store](https://chromewebstore.google.com/detail/pdf-to-audio/fedblkmfahdnefbbbmhpkgliejbflohl?hl=en)[web:214]
- **PDF to MP3 – PDF Audio Reader (ssstudio)** – Android app that converts PDFs to speech with hundreds of offline voices; pitched as "Tired of reading long PDFs? Just listen instead." [PDF to MP3 – PDF Audio Reader - Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.ssstudio.pdftomp3)[web:229]
- **PDF2Audio (Shaozhuang Chen)** – iOS app that turns research papers, ebooks, and reports into "engaging podcasts, focused lectures, or quick summaries" with studio-quality voices. [PDF2Audio – App Store](https://apps.apple.com/tr/app/pdf2audio/id6762536249)[web:226]
- **PDF to MP3 : Turn PDF to Audio (DigitalOfficePro)** – Android app; AI-powered conversion from PDFs to high-quality audio; multiple voice styles and multi-language support. [PDF to MP3 : Turn PDF to Audio - Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.dop.pdftomp3)[web:230]
- **Narrator (PDF to Audio Reader)** – Android app; "convert your PDFs to an audiobook" with simple PDF→audio conversion. [Narrator (PDF to Audio Reader) - Google Play](https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.zalkube.narrator&hl=en_CA)[web:220]
- **PDF to Audio – PDF Converter (Rajaram Bhaskaran)** – iOS app designed to convert work documents, educational materials, and books into "crystal-clear audio files." [PDF to Audio - PDF Converter - App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/pdf-to-audio-pdf-converter/id6456017600)[web:221]
- **PDF2Audio Web Platform** – browser-based AI converter to transform PDFs into human-like audio for learning, accessibility, and productivity. [PDF2Audio AI](https://www.pdf2audio.ai/it)[web:231]
- **ElevenReader** – AI read-aloud app from ElevenLabs; transforms documents, PDFs, books, websites, or scanned pages into natural audio; has a student/educator program and is explicitly pitched for studying. [ElevenReader for Students & Educators](https://elevenreader.io/students)[web:223]
## ADHD and Neurodivergent UX Design References
These sources support ADHD-friendly UX, cognitive accessibility, and emotionally safe design.
- **How to design for ADHD and neurodiversity in UX** – explains how to reduce cognitive load across layout, navigation, and content structure. [Welcoming Web – Designing for ADHD and Neurodiversity](https://welcomingweb.com/learn/designing-for-neurodiversity-adhd-ux)[web:116]
- **ADHD Accessibility — Building UIs That Work for Everyone (Including Me)** – personal, practical article on sensory overload and inclusive UI patterns. [ADHD-Friendly UI Checklist](https://medium.com/@sterling.benjamin/adhd-friendly-ui-checklistadhd-accessibility-building-uis-that-work-for-everyone-including-me)[web:110]
- **The Principles of Neurodivergent UX Design Every Designer Should Know** – introduces neurodivergent UX design with cognitive accessibility at the center, including progressive disclosure. [Neurodivergent UX Design – Accessibility Checker](https://www.accessibilitychecker.org/blog/neurodivergent-ux-design/)[web:111]
- **16 UX Principles to Truly Include Neurodivergent Users** – broad principles for designing for ADHD, autism, and other neurotypes. [Design Bootcamp – 16 UX Principles](https://medium.com/design-bootcamp/beyond-compliance-16-ux-principles-to-truly-include-neurodivergent-users-e7d3ff779665)[web:118]
- **Neurodiversity and UX: Essential Resources for Cognitive Accessibility** – curated resource list and framing for cognitive-friendly UX. [Stephanie Walter – Neurodiversity and UX](https://stephaniewalter.design/blog/neurodiversity-and-ux-essential-resources-for-cognitive-accessibility/)[web:193]
- **ADHD UX Thesis (Jönköping University)** – indicates that participants with ADHD experienced significantly lower cognitive load and higher satisfaction when using an ADHD-tailored prototype interface. [Thesis – ADHD and Cognitive Load](https://hj.diva-portal.org/smash/get/diva2:2069190/FULLTEXT01.pdf)[web:183]
- **What Neurodivergence Taught Me About UX and Emotional Safety** – talk on emotional friction and how to design inclusive, sensory-friendly UX for neurodivergent users. [YouTube – Emotional Safety UX Talk](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3w-g5hdDqmo)[web:222]
## Neuro-Affirming Language and Communications Guides
These references guide respectful, neuro-affirming language for ADHD and neurodivergent users.
- **Neuro-Affirming Language Quality Assurance Checklist (NAIT)** – concrete dos and don’ts for online resources, emphasizing avoiding deficit-only language and stigmatizing terms. [NAIT Neuro-Affirming Language Checklist](https://nait.scot/wp-content/uploads/2025/08/NAIT-Neuro-affirming-Language-Checklist-for-Online-Resources-2025.pdf)[web:187]
- **ADHD Language Guide (SAIT)** – guidance on how to talk about ADHD respectfully and accurately, framing ADHD as a neurotype and focusing on strengths and supports. [SAIT ADHD Language Guide](https://www.sait.ca/assets/documents/about-sait/community-and-belonging/adhd-language-guide.pdf)[web:194]
- **Neuroinclusive Communications Guide** – high-level principles for inclusive communications and neuroinclusive language. [Neuroinclusive Communications Guide](https://cdn.harper-adams.ac.uk/document/page/1358_Neuroinclusive-Communications-Guide-JIC.pdf)[web:196]
- **Neurodiverse Users: UX Writing for Inclusive Digital Worlds** – UX writing guidance for neurodiverse audiences, focusing on clarity, emotional safety, and inclusive tone. [Design Bootcamp – Neurodiverse UX Writing](https://medium.com/design-bootcamp/neurodiverse-users-ux-writing-for-inclusive-digital-worlds-188bc8e693e7)[web:192]
## How to Use This Document (Future You)
This section is for the future version of you who opens this file later and thinks: “Okay, what do I actually do with this?”
### 1. When you’re planning product direction
Use this doc to answer:
- Who is already doing “PDF → audio / reading → audio”?
- What do they emphasize (features, voices, studying, accessibility)?
- Where can you position differently (ADHD focus, emotional safety, calm UX, better voices)?
Steps:
- Read the **Direct Competitors** list once.
- Pick 2–3 competitors and visit their sites or app store pages.
- Write down:
- what they do well,
- what feels overwhelming or generic,
- what they ignore about ADHD and neurodivergent UX.
### 2. When you’re making UX or product decisions
Use the **ADHD & Neurodivergent UX Design References** section as justification and guardrails.
For example:
- When you’re deciding how many options to show on a screen, refer to the neurodivergent UX and cognitive load articles.[web:111][web:118][web:193]
- When you’re designing progress states or reducing overwhelm, revisit the emotional safety talk and ADHD UI checklists.[web:110][web:183][web:222]
Steps:
- Before changing a big flow (landing, signup, upload, player), pick 1–2 links from the UX section and skim them.
- Then make your decision and note in your design doc which links influenced that decision.
This keeps every big choice tied to actual research, not just vibes.
### 3. When you’re writing copy or blog posts
Use the **Neuro-Affirming Language & Communications Guides** as your tone bible.
For product copy:
- Check your error messages, onboarding text, and landing page claims against the NAIT and SAIT guides.[web:187][web:194][web:196]
- Make sure you’re describing ADHD as a neurotype, not a defect.
For blog posts:
- Use the language guides and UX writing articles to shape your tone and structure.[web:192]
- Turn key ideas into articles like:
- “Why ADHD-friendly UX makes every student’s life easier.”
- “Writing error messages that don’t make ADHD users feel broken.”
Steps:
- Before finishing any major copy (landing hero, pricing, “for students” page), skim at least one language guide.
- Fix any phrases that sound like blame, deficit, or “normal vs ADHD.”
### 4. When you’re stuck or overwhelmed
If you feel lost:
- Don’t try to read everything.
- Instead, choose **one competitor link** and **one UX or language link**.
- Read just those two and then decide:
- “What’s one small improvement I can ship this week?”
The goal of this document is not to be a textbook; it’s a **toolbox**.
Each time you open it, grab one or two tools, use them, and close it again.
